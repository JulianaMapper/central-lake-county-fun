About Central Lake County Fun

Home › About

Every parent wants their kids to have a great summer. But finding the right events — the free ones, the ones that fit your kid's age, the ones actually happening nearby — takes hours of clicking through library websites, park district calendars, and Facebook groups.

This site exists to take that invisible load off your plate. We believe every kid in Lake County deserves a summer full of joy, discovery, and connection — and that it shouldn't require a spreadsheet to make that happen.

We built Central Lake County Fun to be the one place a parent can go to see what's happening this week, plan ahead for the summer, and actually show up. No subscriptions, no login, no ads — just the information you need.

What's included

Library EventsStorytimes, STEM, crafts, summer reading programs
Park DistrictsFree programs, splash pads, nature activities
Forest PreservesNature walks, wildlife programs, outdoor exploration
Community EventsFestivals, concerts, village programming

How we find events

Events are collected using web scraping tools that pull directly from library and park district calendars across Central Lake County. We spot-check events for accuracy and remove duplicates, out-of-area listings, and events not relevant to families with young children.

We focus on events that are free or low-cost, geared toward children (especially preschool and elementary age), and within a reasonable drive of the Grayslake area.

Always verify before you go. Event details — times, registration requirements, cancellations — can change without notice. We encourage you to click through to the original event page and confirm with the organizer, especially for events requiring registration.

Who made this

This site was made by a Lake County mom who got tired of maintaining a personal spreadsheet of summer events and thought: other families need this too. It's a community resource, not a business — just a neighbor trying to help.

If you found it useful, a small contribution helps keep it going. And if you know of events we missed, feel free to reach out!
